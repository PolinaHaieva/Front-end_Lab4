Barcelona — a city in Spain, the capital of the autonomous region of Catalonia and the province of Barcelona. The city, with an area of 100.4 km², has a population of 1,611,822 (2013). The Barcelona metropolitan area (the city and its suburbs) has 4,604,000 inhabitants (2013)[12]. The city is the second most populous in Spain after the capital, Madrid. Barcelona lies on a part of the Mediterranean coast (Catalan: la costa de la mar Mediterrània) called Costa del Maresme, north of Costa Brava and south of Costa del Garraf. It is an important seaport. The city is 160 km from the Pyrenees. Barcelona has hosted World Exhibitions in 1888 and 1929, the Summer Olympics in 1992, the World Cultural Forum in 2004, and other world-class events.
